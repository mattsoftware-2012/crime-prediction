About Crime Prediction

Crime prediction technology has evolved significantly over the years. Here's a brief history of how predictive policing and crime forecasting has developed.

1960s: Early Statistical Models

The first attempts at crime prediction used basic statistical models to identify crime hotspots based on historical data.

1990s: Geographic Profiling

Researchers began developing geographic profiling techniques to predict where serial offenders might strike next based on their past behavior.

2000s: COMPSTAT

The NYPD implemented COMPSTAT, a data-driven management system that used crime statistics to allocate police resources more effectively.

2010s: Predictive Policing Software

Commercial predictive policing software like PredPol emerged, using machine learning to forecast where crimes were likely to occur.

2020s: AI-Powered Systems

Modern systems now incorporate artificial intelligence, deep learning, and multiple data sources (social media, weather, events) for more accurate predictions.

Our Approach
